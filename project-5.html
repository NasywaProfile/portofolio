Project Overview

"Vokantin" is a UI/UX project developed as part of an Information Systems Management study to digitize daily canteen operations. The project involved crafting 28 sequential, high-fidelity design screens that map out the complete user journey. The interface specifically focuses on providing clear performance dashboards, seamless financial tracking for income and expenses, precise stock control, and structured approval workflows to modernize how canteen vendors manage their business.

The Problem

Traditional canteen operations often rely on manual, paper-based systems for recording daily transactions, tracking inventory, and managing expenses. This lack of a centralized digital system makes it difficult to monitor financial performance accurately, leads to stock discrepancies, and creates inefficient, time-consuming processes. Vendors needed a structured, easy-to-use digital solution to replace messy manual reporting and provide clear visibility into their daily operational metrics.

Methodology

The design process was rooted in user-centric principles, beginning with a direct field interview with a real canteen vendor to gather authentic data, analyze existing business processes, and identify core pain points. Based on these real-world insights, comprehensive user flows were mapped out. This was followed by designing the 28 sequential screens to visualize the digital solution. Finally, the designs were structured into a presentation to validate the usability of the interfaces and demonstrate the efficiency of the newly proposed operational workflows.
